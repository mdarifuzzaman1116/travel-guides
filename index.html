🛡️ U.S. State Dept Travel Advisory

Level 2 Exercise Increased Caution — Dominican Republic

Violent crime (robbery, assault) is a concern throughout the DR. However, tourist resort zones are generally better policed and more secure. Same advisory level as France, Italy & Germany.

  • 🚨 Emergency: 911
  • 👮 Tourist Police (POLITUR): 809-222-2026
  • 🇺🇸 U.S. Embassy: 809-567-7775
  • 📱 Register your trip: STEP Program

Don't display valuables, avoid using phones openly on streets, travel with a partner.

🏥 CDC Health Advisory

Health — Dominican Republic

  • ✅ No vaccinations required from the U.S.
  • 💉 Recommended: Hepatitis A, Typhoid, routine vaccines up to date
  • 🦟 Mosquito risks: Dengue, Zika, Chikungunya — use DEET repellent
  • 💧 Drink bottled water only — avoid ice outside of resorts
  • 🏥 Travel insurance with emergency medical evacuation strongly recommended
  • 👶 Infant Zika note: Dress baby in long sleeves at dawn/dusk, use mosquito nets over stroller/bassinet
📋 Entry Requirements

U.S. Citizens Entering Dominican Republic

  • ✅ No visa required for stays under 30 days
  • 📘 Passport valid for 6 months from arrival date
  • 🎟️ $10 Tourist Card — included in airfare
  • 🛫 $20 Departure tax — included in airfare
  • 🔄 Round-trip ticket required at boarding (airlines verify)

💡 Insider Tips
✈️

Book JetBlue early. It's the only nonstop from JFK to POP. April is peak season and seats fill fast.

🏨

All-inclusive is ideal with an infant. Eliminates stress of finding safe food/water for baby — everything is handled.

👮

POLITUR tourist police are visible in resort zones — friendly and helpful. Don't hesitate to approach them.

📱

Pocket your phone when walking city streets. Don't use it openly — pickpocket risk. Fine inside resorts.

💵

Tip in USD. $1–2 for housekeeping daily, $5 for tours. Small bills are highly appreciated by staff.

🦟

Mosquito-proof baby at dawn/dusk. Long sleeves, baby-safe DEET wipes, and mosquito net over the stroller.

🚗

Hire a local driver for day trips (Damajagua, Cabarete). Often safer and cheaper than self-driving unfamiliar roads.

🌅

Best sunrise: Malecón boardwalk at 6:30 AM — serene, stroller-friendly, and magical before crowds arrive.

☀️

April timing is excellent. End of the dry season — mostly sunny before the May/June rainy period kicks in.

🍫

Buy Dominican cacao, coffee & cigars as souvenirs — all world-class and inexpensive. Del Oro is great for chocolate.

👶 Infant Travel Notes

👶 Your Daughter · 8 Months Old at Travel Time

  • ✅ Lap infant on all flights — under 24 months, no seat purchase required
  • 💺 JetBlue lap infant fee: ~$100 each way (international) → ~$200 round trip total for baby
  • 🛏️ Request a bassinet seat (bulkhead row) when booking — essential for 3h 48m nonstop
  • 🏨 All-inclusive resorts typically have cribs/pack-n-plays available on request — confirm at booking
  • 🎫 Baby needs to be on the eTicket — list her under your family entry form
  • 🌡️ Apply baby SPF 50+ — she's old enough for sunscreen at 8 months
  • 🦟 Dress her in long sleeves at dawn/dusk for Zika/Dengue mosquito protection
  • 💧 Use bottled water only for formula mixing — even at resorts
  • 🧳 Pack: travel crib, lightweight stroller, baby carrier, formula/baby food (declare at customs), basic infant first-aid kit
  • 📘 Baby needs her own U.S. Passport — apply early if not done!
