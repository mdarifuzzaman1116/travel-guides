🛡 U.S. State Dept Travel Advisory

🇺🇸 Jamaica — Updated January 17, 2026 Level 2 — Exercise Increased Caution
Downgraded from Level 3 after Hurricane Melissa recovery. Tourist areas in Montego Bay (Hip Strip and Rose Hall) and Negril are fully operational. Crime risk exists in non-tourist areas.
  • Avoid public buses and driving between cities at night
  • Do not wander into non-tourist neighborhoods after dark
  • Stick to arranged resort excursions, not street-level tours
  • Keep a low profile and avoid flashy jewelry
  • Some areas in western Jamaica still recovering from Hurricane Melissa — confirm resort availability before booking
  • Certain parishes (St James inner city areas) carry Level 4 — Do Not Travel designations
U.S. Embassy Kingston: +1 876-702-6000

🏥 CDC Health Advisory

💉 Jamaica Health Notices Standard Precautions
No special vaccinations required for U.S. citizens. Recommended vaccines:
  • Routine vaccines up to date (MMR, Tdap, flu, COVID)
  • Hepatitis A — recommended for all travelers
  • Hepatitis B — recommended
  • Typhoid — if eating outside resort
  • Dengue fever risk is present — use DEET repellent, especially at dawn/dusk
  • Drink bottled water only — hotel tap water is safe at major resorts but use caution otherwise
For your infant: Consult your pediatrician before travel. No special vaccines required for infants at this age beyond routine schedule. Bring infant-grade DEET-free insect repellent and sunscreen.

📄 Entry Requirements for U.S. Citizens

Passport
Valid U.S. passport required. Must be valid for duration of stay.
Visa
No visa required for U.S. citizens. Tourist stays up to 90 days.
Immigration Form
Complete Jamaica immigration form (C5) online before arrival for faster processing at MBJ.
Infant Travel
Both parents traveling together — bring baby's U.S. passport and birth certificate. No additional consent needed when both parents present.
Return Ticket
Proof of onward or return travel required. Have your return flight confirmation accessible.
Airport Departure Tax
Included in airline ticket price. No separate payment needed at airport.

💡 Insider Tips — Montego Bay

✈️
Fly Tuesday or Wednesday
May midweek JFK–MBJ flights are consistently cheapest. Avoid Friday–Sunday departures which run $50–$150 more per person.
🏨
Book Rose Hall Area
Rose Hall resorts are 15 minutes from MBJ airport. Newer properties, quieter than the Hip Strip, and still have all amenities on-site.
👶
Request Crib in Advance
Email your resort directly to request a crib, pack-n-play, and baby-safe room setup before arrival. Most resorts provide this free of charge.
💵
USD is King
USD is accepted almost everywhere in MoBay tourist zones. No need to exchange much cash. Bring $100–$200 in small bills for tips and street food.
🚕
Use JUTA Taxis Only
JUTA taxis are the official licensed tourist taxis. Red license plates. Ask your resort to arrange them. Never take unmarked taxis off the street.
🌞
Book Excursions Through Resort
Resort concierge uses vetted, insured operators. Slightly more expensive but safer and more reliable than booking random operators on the street.
🏖️
Stay Within Your Resort's Zone
The Rose Hall resort corridor is self-contained and very safe. Stick to the resort strip, arranged excursions, and well-known tourist spots during the day.
🎵
Reggae Sundays
Many resorts host live reggae and cultural nights on Sundays. Check with your resort for their weekly entertainment schedule. Great authentic experience without leaving the property.
☀️
Sun is Intense in May
Caribbean UV is extremely strong even on cloudy days. Reapply SPF 50+ every 90 minutes. For the baby, use baby-specific mineral sunscreen and shade them between 10 AM–3 PM.
🦟
Mosquito Protection
Use DEET-based repellent for adults at dawn and dusk. For the baby use DEET-free picaridin or citronella-based repellent. Pack lightweight long sleeves for evenings.
📱
Get WhatsApp Before You Go
Jamaican businesses, taxis, and excursion operators primarily communicate via WhatsApp. Having it installed makes logistics much easier on the ground.
🍋
Try the Local Rum
Appleton Estate and Wray & Nephew are Jamaica's top rums — available everywhere for a fraction of US prices. Pick up a bottle at the airport duty-free on the way home.

👶 Infant Travel Notes

👶
Your daughter will be 8–9 months old at travel time — Lap Infant Status
Flight Seat
Flies as lap infant free of charge. She is under 24 months so no purchased seat required. Bring her U.S. passport and birth certificate.
Infant Fare
On international flights, lap infant fare is approximately 10% of adult base fare (~$35–$45 each way). Confirm with your airline at booking.
Crib at Resort
Request a travel crib / pack-n-play from the resort before arrival. All major Montego Bay all-inclusives provide these free of charge with advance notice.
Baby Food & Formula
Bring all formula, pouches, and purees from home. TSA allows baby food through security in any quantity. Jamaican resorts may not carry specific US brands.
Beach Safety
Shade her between 10 AM–3 PM. Use mineral sunscreen SPF 50+ for babies. Keep her out of direct sun as much as possible. The calm resort beach water is safe for splashing.
Stroller Advice
Bring a lightweight umbrella stroller. Montego Bay resort paths are paved. A baby carrier/wrap is also very useful for excursions where strollers are impractical (river rafting, etc.).
Health Precautions
Consult pediatrician before travel. No special vaccines required for infants at this age. Use only bottled water for mixing formula. Bring infant Tylenol, gas drops, and first aid kit.
Sleep Schedule
Jamaica is in Eastern Standard Time (same as New York in winter, 1 hour behind in summer depending on DST). Minimal time zone adjustment needed from Brooklyn.
