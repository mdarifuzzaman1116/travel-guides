U.S. State Dept Travel Advisory
Level 2 - Exercise Increased Caution

Level 2 is the same rating as France, Italy, and Costa Rica. Crime is the concern, not terrorism. Tourist areas are safe and welcoming.

  • Crime: Petty theft in Bridgetown; violent crime rare in tourist areas
  • Beach safety: Atlantic east coast has strong riptides - swim on the calm Caribbean west side only
  • Road safety: Drive on the left; roundabouts common; roads slippery after rain
  • Medical: Queen Elizabeth Hospital in Bridgetown - best in the region for a Caribbean island
  • LGBTQ+: Same-sex relations decriminalized in 2022 - generally welcoming atmosphere

CDC Health Advisory
  • Hepatitis A: Recommended for all travelers
  • Typhoid: Recommended especially eating outside resorts
  • Dengue: Present - use DEET repellent at dawn and dusk
  • Zika: Low but present - consult doctor if planning future pregnancies

Insider Tips
🏈
Swim West Coast Only
The calm Caribbean west side (Paynes Bay, Brownes Beach) is safe for babies. The Atlantic east side has dangerous riptides - scenic to look at, never swim there.
🚌
Buses Are Cheap
Government buses cost ~$0.75 per ride and cover the whole island. If left-side driving feels stressful, buses are a fun and reliable alternative.
💧
Tap Water Is Safe
Barbados has one of the best water quality records in the Caribbean. Tap water is completely safe - a big plus with a baby. No bottled water stress.
🐒
Green Monkeys Everywhere
Wild green monkeys roam freely on the east coast and wildlife reserve. Keep your baby at a safe distance - they can snatch food and may bite.
📷
Cover Up in Town
No beachwear in town, shops, or restaurants. Pack a light breathable cover-up or shirt for off-beach exploring. Barbados has a conservative dress code in public.
🌧
Rain Stops Fast
September showers usually last 20-30 minutes then sunshine returns. Bring a light poncho and wait it out. Mornings are almost always the clearest part of the day.
🦘
Dengue Risk - Use Repellent
Dengue mosquitoes are active especially in wet season. DEET for adults; Picaridin spray on baby's clothing. Extra vigilant at dawn and dusk.
💵
USD Widely Accepted
USD accepted almost everywhere at the fixed 2:1 BDS rate. ATMs dispense BDS dollars. Both currencies work everywhere on the island.
🖐
Bajans Are Famously Friendly
Barbadians are warm, helpful, and proud of their island. Always greet people with good morning or good afternoon before any conversation - it's expected and appreciated.
🎹
Sunday Is Social Day
Sundays in Barbados are lively - Lobster Alive lunch, rum shops gathering, relaxed island pace. Lobster Alive Sunday lunch with live music is unmissable.
🛡
No Hurricane Since 1955
Barbados sits at the southeastern edge of the Caribbean hurricane zone. Storms historically veer north before reaching the island. Lowest hurricane risk in the region.
🎵
Rihanna's Homeland
Barbados is Rihanna's birthplace and they are very proud of it. A national holiday was named in her honor. Look for Clara's ice cream near Oistins - her childhood favorite.
Infant Travel Notes

Your daughter will be approximately 13 months old in September 2026

  • Lap infant: Under 24 months, no seat needed on flights (~10% adult fare each way)
  • Documents: Birth certificate required at immigration; if solo parent bring notarized consent letter
  • Best beaches for baby: Paynes Bay (calmest water), Brownes Beach (free), Crane Beach south pool (shallow and protected)
  • Tap water safe to drink: One of very few Caribbean islands where this is true - huge win for mixing baby food and formula
  • No malaria risk in Barbados: Much simpler pediatric precautions than other Caribbean destinations
  • Bug protection: Picaridin spray on clothing not DEET directly on skin. Long sleeves and pants at dawn and dusk.
  • Sugar Bay creche: If staying at Sugar Bay, dedicated creche for babies 4 months to 3 years with trained staff. Gives you and your wife proper beach time.
  • Stroller: Bridgetown and west coast towns are stroller-friendly. East coast rougher - baby carrier preferred.
  • Sun protection: Barbados sun is intense. Baby SPF 50+, long-sleeve rash guard, shade breaks every 30-40 minutes.
  • Healthcare: Queen Elizabeth Hospital in Bridgetown is the best hospital in the region for a Caribbean island - genuine reassurance if anything goes wrong.
