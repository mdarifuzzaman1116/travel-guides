Insider Tips
💳
Cards & Cash
Visa/Mastercard widely accepted. Discover not accepted anywhere. Card machines fail on spotty internet — carry $200–$300 USD cash. Credit cards may add a surcharge; ask upfront.
📱
Phone / SIM
Buy an eSIM at Airalo.com before departure (~$9.50/1GB/7 days). Works on iPhone XS and newer. Or pick up SmartBelize / Digicell SIM in Belize City — bring passport.
🌙
No Night Driving
U.S. Embassy warns against driving between cities after dark. No road lighting, speed bumps without warning, animals on roads. All inter-city travel must be done before sunset.
🏥
Medical Reality
Advanced care is scarce even in Belize City. Serious injuries often require evacuation to Mexico or US. Travel insurance with evacuation is non-negotiable with an infant.
🪸
Reef-Safe Sunscreen
Zinc oxide or titanium dioxide only near the water. Chemical sunscreens (oxybenzone) damage coral and are restricted near the reef.
🚗
No Uber / Lyft
No ride-share apps in Belize. Use licensed taxis (agree price before getting in), water taxis for inter-caye travel, or Tropic Air / Maya Island Air domestic flights.
🌊
Seagrass Beaches
The reef breaks waves offshore — beaches are calm but often have seagrass. Secret Beach and Placencia are cleanest. Swim off docks for clearest water.
🦟
Bug Protection Daily
Dengue is the concern in 2026. DEET or Picaridin every morning and at dusk. For infant: Picaridin 20% on clothing only. Never use DEET directly on infant skin.
🛫
Build Buffer Time
Domestic flights and water taxis run on a relaxed schedule. Allow 2–3 hours buffer after your international arrival before any connecting transport to islands.
⚡
Power Outages
Brief outages can happen in dry season but are less common in April. All 4-star and 5-star resorts have generators. Bring a portable power bank.
