2026 NYC Cherry Blossom Timeline

Late March 2026

Okame Cherry Blossoms — First to Bloom 🌸

The rosy-pink Okame variety blooms first in NYC. Spotted at the Bridle Path (west side of the Reservoir) in Central Park, and at Flushing Meadows-Corona Park near the Unisphere. Usually the first to pop — late March to early April.

~April 5–15, 2026 ← YOU ARE HERE

Yoshino Cherry Blossoms — The Famous White-Pink Clouds ⭐

The iconic white/light-pink Yoshinos are the most celebrated. NYC peak expected around April 5–15 based on DC's March 26 peak and historical 2-week lag. Check trackers daily — this window is only 5–7 days!

~April 14–30, 2026

Kwanzan / Kanzan — The Fluffy Deep-Pink Pompoms 💗

The showstopping double-petaled Kwanzan bloom later and last a bit longer. Brooklyn Botanic Garden's Cherry Esplanade is famous for these. BBG's Hanami Nights events (April 21–24) are timed for Kwanzan peak. Best late April bet!

May 3 & May 10, 2026

BBG Weekends in Bloom Events 🎉

Even if peak blossoms are gone, BBG hosts spring bloom events on May 3 and 10. The garden also features magnolias, tree peonies, wisteria, crabapples, and irises throughout spring.

🌸 Brooklyn Botanic Garden Best in NYC

The crown jewel of NYC cherry blossom viewing. Over 200 cherry trees including the famous Cherry Esplanade — a tunnel of deep-pink Kwanzan blossoms. Also features the Japanese Hill-and-Pond Garden. Has its own CherryWatch live tracker. Hosts Hanami Nights (April 21–24) — a magical evening event under the lit blossoms.

Admission: $22 adults, $16 seniors/students, free under 12 (great for baby!)
Hours: Tue–Thu 8am–8:30pm, Fri–Sun 8am–6pm, Closed Mondays
Subway: Prospect Park (B/Q) or Eastern Parkway (2/3)

🌳 Central Park Most Iconic

Hundreds of cherry trees spread across the park between 72nd and 96th Streets. The Central Park Conservancy runs a real-time bloom tracker — color-coded by zone. Top spots: Cherry Hill (most popular, frames the San Remo building), JKO Reservoir (east & west sides), Pilgrim Hill, Great Lawn, and Sheep Meadow.

Varieties: Kwanzan, Higan, Yoshino, Okame — staggered season means multiple chances to catch bloom.
Admission: Free 🎉
Tip: Arrive before 8am for empty frames + golden light

🚡 Roosevelt Island Hidden Gem

A small island in the East River between Manhattan and Queens. The waterfront promenade is lined with Yoshino, Kwanzan, and Okame cherry trees with open Manhattan skyline views. The largest concentration of Kwanzan trees is on Cherry Tree Walk (West Loop Road) near Cornell Tech on the south end. Take the iconic tram from 59th St & 2nd Ave — a 4-minute ride 250 feet above the East River. Arrive at sunrise for the famous tree-tunnel shot without crowds.

Admission: Free (Tram: $2.90 OMNY/MetroCard each way)

🗺 Bonus Spots to Explore Bonus

Flushing Meadows-Corona Park (Queens): Okame trees near the Unisphere — first to bloom in NYC! Iconic photo with the giant steel globe.

Insider Tips — Cherry Blossom Hunting NYC

⏰

Arrive at Sunrise

Roosevelt Island, Cherry Hill, and Gantry Plaza are dramatically different before 8 AM. After sunrise: beat the crowds and get clean frames.

📡

Check Trackers Daily

BBG's CherryWatch and the Central Park tracker update frequently. Peak can shift overnight — check every morning during your trip.

🌧

Go Before Rain

Wind and rain are the enemy. One storm can end peak bloom. If rain is forecast for tomorrow, go today — no exceptions.

📅

Weekdays Are Better

BBG and Cherry Hill are extremely crowded on peak weekends. Go Tuesday–Thursday morning for the best experience with your baby stroller.

🗓

Stagger Your Visits

Yoshino peaks first (early April), Kwanzan peaks later (mid-late April). Plan 2 separate visits to catch both. NYC is forgiving — multiple opportunities!

🚡

Take the Roosevelt Island Tram

Don't miss the 4-minute tram ride from 59th St & 2nd Ave — it's an NYC bucket-list experience and delivers you right to the cherry trees.

📸

Best Photo Spots

Cherry Hill (San Remo building backdrop), Roosevelt Island (skyline + tram), JKO Reservoir east side, Washington Square Park arch with magnolias.

🎟

Book BBG Tickets Early

Hanami Nights (April 21–24) and BBG evening events sell out fast. Buy tickets 2–3 weeks in advance. Regular admission is walk-in or online.

🌸

Follow the Staggered Season

Early April: Okame & Yoshino. Mid-April: Yoshino peak. Late April: Kwanzan. The 5-week season gives you multiple shots at catching something beautiful.

🏙

Prospect Park + BBG Combo

BBG is right next to Prospect Park (Brooklyn). Do both in one day — Prospect Park is free and also has cherry trees throughout.

📱

Instagram Channel for Live Updates

Follow NYC photographer Nire Donahue's Instagram channel for daily status updates during bloom season — fastest real-time conditions anywhere.

🎋

Sakura Matsuri Festival

BBG's annual Sakura Matsuri celebrates Japanese cherry blossom culture with traditional music, dance, and food. Check BBG's 2026 date announcement.

👶 Infant Travel Notes — Your Daughter (8 months at April 2026)

Stroller-Friendly: All NYC cherry blossom spots are stroller-accessible. Central Park, BBG, and Roosevelt Island all have paved paths.

BBG Tip: Rent a wagon or bring a carrier — BBG's paths can get crowded. Baby carrier is great for photos under the blossoms!

Roosevelt Island Tram: Strollers are welcome on the tram — it's a smooth, short ride and very exciting even for infants.

Weather: April NYC can be chilly in the morning (50s°F). Dress baby in layers — hat, light jacket. Afternoons warm up to 60–65°F.

Feeding/Diaper: BBG has restroom facilities. Central Park has restrooms near all major areas. Roosevelt Island has limited facilities — pack extra.

Best visit time: Mid-morning (9–11am) after morning feed — baby is usually happiest and parks are less crowded than afternoons.
